RECITALS

WHEREAS:

A. The Controller has engaged the Processor to provide the Inland Shipping Onboarding System ("Services") as described in the Principal Agreement for the Dutch inland shipping (binnenvaart) sector;

B. The provision of Services involves the Processing of Personal Data on behalf of the Controller;

C. The Parties wish to ensure that such Processing complies with Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (General Data Protection Regulation, "GDPR"), the Dutch Implementation Act (Uitvoeringswet AVG, "UAVG"), and other applicable data protection laws;

D. This DPA sets out the terms governing the Processing of Personal Data by the Processor on behalf of the Controller.

NOW THEREFORE, the Parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITIONS

In this DPA, the following terms shall have the meanings set out below:

Term Definition
"Applicable Laws" All laws and regulations applicable to the Processing of Personal Data under this DPA, including GDPR and UAVG
"Data Subject" An identified or identifiable natural person whose Personal Data is Processed
"Personal Data" Any information relating to a Data Subject that is Processed by the Processor on behalf of the Controller
"Personal Data Breach" A breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data
"Processing" Any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, and deletion
"Sub-processor" Any third party engaged by the Processor to Process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller
"Supervisory Authority" The Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens) or other competent authority

2. PROCESSING OF PERSONAL DATA

2.1 Processor's Obligations

The Processor shall:

a) Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, as set out in Schedule 1;

b) Ensure that persons authorized to Process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality;

c) Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, as described in Schedule 2;

d) Not engage another processor without prior specific or general written authorization from the Controller;

e) Assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with obligations pursuant to Articles 32-36 GDPR;

f) At the Controller's choice, delete or return all Personal Data after the end of the provision of Services;

g) Make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA;

h) Allow for and contribute to audits conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller.

2.2 Controller's Obligations

The Controller shall:

a) Ensure that it has all necessary lawful bases for the Processing of Personal Data;

b) Provide clear and lawful Processing instructions to the Processor;

c) Ensure the accuracy of Personal Data provided to the Processor;

d) Comply with all Applicable Laws relating to its Processing of Personal Data;

e) Respond promptly to any Processor requests for clarification regarding Processing instructions.

b) Provide the Controller with at least 30 days prior notice before engaging new Sub-processors;

c) Ensure each Sub-processor is bound by data protection obligations no less protective than those in this DPA;

d) Remain fully liable for any Sub-processor's performance.

4.3 Objection Rights

The Controller may object to the engagement of a new Sub-processor within 14 days of notification. If the Parties cannot resolve the objection, the Controller may terminate the affected Services.

5. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

5.1 Assistance with Requests

The Processor shall:

a) Promptly notify the Controller of any request received directly from a Data Subject;

b) Not respond to such request except on documented instructions from the Controller;

c) Assist the Controller in responding to Data Subject requests within the following timeframes:

Request Type Processor Response Time Method of Assistance
Access Within 5 business days Provide data export
Rectification Within 3 business days Update systems
Erasure Within 5 business days Delete/anonymize data
Restriction Within 2 business days Restrict processing
Portability Within 5 business days Export in standard format
Objection Within 2 business days Cease processing

5.2 Technical Capabilities

The Processor maintains the following technical capabilities to support Data Subject rights:

  • Automated data export functionality (JSON/CSV formats)
  • Granular deletion capabilities
  • Data correction interfaces
  • Processing restriction flags
  • Consent management system
  • Audit trail of all data operations

6. PERSONAL DATA BREACH

6.1 Breach Notification

The Processor shall:

a) Notify the Controller without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach;

b) Provide the following information:

  • Nature of the breach
  • Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected
  • Categories and approximate number of Personal Data records affected
  • Likely consequences of the breach
  • Measures taken or proposed to address the breach
  • Contact details for more information

6.2 Breach Response

The Processor shall:

a) Immediately take steps to contain and remediate the breach;

b) Cooperate fully with the Controller's investigation;

c) Not publicly disclose the breach without the Controller's prior written consent;

d) Document all breaches, regardless of notification requirements;

e) Conduct a post-incident review and implement lessons learned.

  • Incident response procedures

  • Regular security assessments and audits

8. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

8.1 Data Localization

All Personal Data shall be stored and Processed exclusively within the European Economic Area (EEA), specifically:

  • Primary data center: Frankfurt, Germany
  • Backup location: Frankfurt, Germany
  • No transfers outside the EEA without explicit Controller approval

8.2 Transfer Mechanisms

If international transfers become necessary:

a) The Processor shall notify the Controller in advance;

b) Appropriate safeguards shall be implemented:

  • EU Standard Contractual Clauses
  • Adequacy decisions
  • Binding Corporate Rules
  • Other approved mechanisms under Chapter V GDPR

9. AUDIT AND COMPLIANCE

9.1 Audit Rights

The Controller may:

a) Conduct audits up to once per year with 30 days' notice;

b) Request additional audits following a Personal Data Breach;

c) Appoint an independent third-party auditor (subject to confidentiality);

d) Review the Processor's security certifications and audit reports.

9.2 Processor Obligations

The Processor shall:

a) Cooperate fully with audits;

b) Provide access to relevant facilities, systems, and documentation;

c) Bear its own costs for standard annual audits;

d) Remedy any identified non-compliance at its own expense.

9.3 Certifications

The Processor implements:

  • ISO 27001 controls (operational implementation)
  • Regular security assessments
  • GDPR compliance reviews
  • Security monitoring and incident response

10. DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

10.2 Data Deletion

Upon termination of the Principal Agreement:

a) The Controller may export all Personal Data within 60 days;

b) The Processor shall delete all Personal Data within 30 days after the export period;

c) The Processor shall provide written certification of deletion;

d) Retention for legal compliance shall be permitted with Controller consent.

11. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

11.1 Liability

Each Party's liability shall be as set forth in the Principal Agreement, subject to:

a) The Processor's liability for data protection violations shall not be limited for:

  • Intentional breaches
  • Gross negligence
  • Regulatory fines attributable to Processor's actions

b) The liability cap shall be €5,000,000 per incident or series of related incidents.

11.2 Indemnification

The Processor shall indemnify the Controller against:

a) Regulatory fines resulting from the Processor's breach of this DPA;

b) Third-party claims arising from the Processor's unlawful Processing;

c) Costs incurred due to the Processor's failure to comply with Controller instructions.

12. TERM AND TERMINATION

12.1 Duration

This DPA shall:

a) Come into effect on the Effective Date;

b) Continue for the duration of the Principal Agreement;

c) Survive termination to the extent necessary for data deletion and legal obligations.

12.2 Termination Rights

Either Party may terminate this DPA:

a) Upon material breach with 30 days written notice to cure;

b) Immediately upon insolvency or cessation of business;

c) If required by a Supervisory Authority or court order.

13. MISCELLANEOUS

13.1 Governing Law

This DPA shall be governed by the laws of the Netherlands, without regard to conflict of law principles.

13.2 Dispute Resolution

Disputes shall be resolved through:

a) Good faith negotiations (30 days);

b) Mediation under Netherlands Mediation Institute rules;

c) Arbitration under Netherlands Arbitration Institute rules;

d) Competent courts of Amsterdam as last resort.

13.3 Amendments

This DPA may only be amended in writing, signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

13.4 Severability

If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force.

13.5 Entire Agreement

This DPA, together with the Principal Agreement and Schedules, constitutes the entire agreement regarding data processing.
